Housekeeping / Waste Management

Housekeeping / Waste Management

Responsibilities & General Duties

  • The Housekeeping (HK) team is responsible for maintaining the cleanliness and hygiene of the entire Brigade Meadows Phase 1 community.
  • They ensure that all floors, lobbies, staircases, and common areas are cleaned regularly and kept free from clutter, dust, and waste.
  • HK staff are responsible for ensuring proper waste collection and transport to the designated disposal points within the campus.
  • Wet waste is transported daily to the composting plant located near Club Meadows, usually by 11:00 AM.
  • Housekeeping staff are not allowed to perform any personal chores or tasks for residents, during or outside duty hours.
  • HK staff will remove dead pigeons if it is safe to do so. Live pigeon removal is not permitted.

Waste Segregation Rules

All residents must follow BBMP regulations and society-specific waste segregation policies. Waste must be separated into the following categories before disposal:

  • Wet Waste: Includes cooked and uncooked food, fruit/vegetable peels, etc.
  • Dry Waste: Includes paper, plastic, cardboard, packaging, metal, glass, etc.
  • Reject Waste: Includes sanitary pads, diapers, medical waste, and soiled tissues.
  • E-Waste: Includes batteries, phones, appliances, wires, etc.
  • Mixing wet and dry waste is strictly prohibited and may attract penalties as per society rules.

Waste Collection Schedule

  • Wet Waste & Reject Waste: Collected daily by HK staff.
  • Dry Waste: Collected daily.
  • E-Waste: Must be dropped in the designated E-Bin opposite the basement lift. HK staff will not collect it from flats.
  • Residents must ensure bins are placed outside only during the collection window.

Garbage Bin Etiquette

  • Garbage bins must be placed outside the apartment by 8:30 AM on collection days.
  • Bins should not be placed outside the flat the night before to prevent odor or pest issues.
  • Bins must be placed only in front of the resident's own door and not in front of lifts or other flats.
  • After collection, bins must be taken inside â€” empty bins must not be left in the corridors.
  • If a resident is away, the Estate Manager or HK Supervisor must be informed for special instructions.

Special Waste Handling

  • Renovation debris, construction waste, or scrap will not be collected by the HK team.
  • Residents must make their own arrangements to dispose of construction waste through approved vendors.
  • Accidental debris or broken items (e.g., glass, ceramics) must be safely packaged and disposed of by the resident.
  • In case of exceptional waste quantity (from parties, clear-outs, etc.), prior permission must be taken from the EM office. Extra charges may apply.

Penalties Related to Waste Management

  • Mixing garbage without segregation is a rule violation and is subject to fines.
  • Throwing waste in common areas such as lobbies, lifts, corridors, or staircases is strictly prohibited and will incur penalties.
  • Improper garbage placement or spillage will attract fines and cleaning charges.
  • Repeat violations can lead to increased fines and written warnings from the EC or Estate Office.

Resident Awareness & Cooperation

  • Residents are expected to actively participate in waste reduction and eco-friendly disposal habits.
  • Instructions from HK staff regarding timing, segregation, or bin placement should be respected and followed.
  • Residents should report any issues related to housekeeping to the Estate Management office instead of instructing HK staff directly.
  • Complaints can be addressed formally to the EC or EM team for resolution and action.
