Move-In / Move-Out Policy

Move-In / Move-Out Policy

Application & Documentation

  • Residents (Owners/Tenants) must complete the Move-In/Move-Out Application Form with all fields properly filled.
  • A valid Lease/Rental Agreement must be submitted with the Move-In/Move-Out Application.
  • The tenants listed in the Lease/Rental Agreement must match those in the Application Form.
  • Application must be submitted to the Estate Management (EM) office for verification and approval.

Tenant Verification & Compliance

  • For bachelor tenants, ensure all names are included in the Lease/Rental Agreement.
  • The number of bachelor tenants must comply with the community regulations:
    • 1 bachelor Tenant for 1 BHK
    • 2 bachelor Tenants for 2 BHK
    • 3 bachelor Tenants for 3 BHK
  • Bachelor tenants must submit the Bachelor Tenant Format, signed by all tenants.
  • Tenant details will be verified against the Lease Agreement to ensure compliance with society regulations.
  • The Move-In/Move-Out Application must be fully filled with no missing or incorrect details.

Financial Clearance

  • Verify that all outstanding charges (CAM dues, penalties, or other fees) are cleared before granting permission for Move-In/Move-Out.
  • If outstanding dues exist, tenants must settle them before proceeding with the Move-In or Move-Out.
  • If tenants have any pending payments, the owner must assist them in clearing the pending charges before processing the application.
  • Once financial clearance is obtained, the approval for the move will be processed.

KYC & Security Compliance

  • The EM office will collect KYC documents such as Aadhaar, Passport, or other valid Government-issued ID from each tenant or resident.
  • Prospective tenants must pay Move-In/Move-Out Charges and provide a payment receipt or UTR number as proof of payment in the application form.
  • The copies of the Move-In/Move-Out Form must be shared with the security team to ensure the move is logged and cleared by security before proceeding.
  • The original Move-In/Move-Out form must be submitted to the EM office for records and approval.
  • Any discrepancies or missing documents will result in delays, and the move will be deferred until the required documents are provided.

Vehicle Registration & Parking Stickers

  • Once the Move-In formalities are completed, tenants must submit vehicle registration (RC) copies and a valid driving license to the EM office for verification.
  • Vehicle stickers will be issued only after completing all the Move-In formalities and after proper verification of the documents.
  • Vehicle stickers will be provided based on the number of vehicles allowed per unit according to the society's policy.
  • Ensure that all vehicles registered for Move-In/Move-Out comply with parking and entry regulations set by the society.
  • Vehicles will be allowed to park in designated areas only, and unauthorized parking will incur penalties.

Move-In/Move-Out Time & Day Restrictions

  • Move-in and move-out activities are not allowed on Sundays, Public Holidays, or after 6:00 PM on any working day.
  • Any move activities that take place outside the permitted time will be subject to penalties or a fine.
  • Moves are allowed only during standard operational hours to ensure minimal disruption to the community.
  • Specific time slots for the move-in and move-out should be coordinated with the Estate Management office for approval.
  • Prior notice of at least one day is required before the move is scheduled to ensure that all formalities are in place.

System Update & Final Approvals

  • All resident and tenant details must be updated in the MyGate portal before proceeding with the move.
  • Once all documentation is completed, and payments are verified, final approval must be obtained from the Estate Office before moving activities can commence.
  • The final move is recorded in the MyGate portal for security tracking and access control.
  • The Estate Management office will ensure that the property is fully compliant with the necessary guidelines for the move to proceed smoothly.

Elevator Usage

  • Passenger lifts must not be used for carrying heavy items or during move-in/move-out activities.
  • Residents should use service lifts or carry heavy items via stairs to avoid damaging the lifts.
  • The use of lifts during the move must be coordinated with security personnel to avoid overcrowding or misuse.

Cleanliness & Housekeeping Responsibility

  • During Move-In/Move-Out, all packaging waste, discarded furniture, and materials must be removed by the resident or tenant.
  • Common areas, corridors, or lobbies must not be littered with items during the move.
  • Any waste or debris left behind will result in cleaning fees and penalties, which the tenant or owner will be responsible for.

Penalties & Rule Violations

  • Moving outside permitted hours will result in a fine. This fine will be classified based on the severity of the violation (low, medium, or high).
  • Using common areas, elevators, or damaging society property during the move will incur fines and the deduction of security deposits.
  • Spitting, smoking, or improper garbage disposal during Move-In/Move-Out activities will lead to penalties.
  • Any damage caused to the property, including furniture or infrastructure, will be charged to the moving party, and the costs will be recovered from their security deposit.
